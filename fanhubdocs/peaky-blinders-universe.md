# The Peaky Blinders Universe

> A reference guide to the characters, lore, and story of _Peaky Blinders_ (BBC, 2013–2022), including the feature film _The Immortal Man_ (Netflix, 2026).

---

## The Show

|                    |                                                  |
| ------------------ | ------------------------------------------------ |
| **Network**        | BBC Two (Series 1–4), BBC One (Series 5–6)       |
| **Genre**          | Historical Crime Drama                           |
| **Run**            | 12 September 2013 – 3 April 2022                 |
| **Seasons**        | 6                                                |
| **Total Episodes** | 36                                               |
| **Setting**        | Birmingham, England (Small Heath), and beyond    |
| **Created By**     | Steven Knight                                    |
| **Theme Song**     | _"Red Right Hand"_ — Nick Cave and the Bad Seeds |

**Premise:** In the aftermath of the First World War, Tommy Shelby — a decorated veteran of mixed Irish Traveller and Romani heritage — transforms a street gang in the working-class Small Heath district of Birmingham into a vast criminal empire. The story follows the Shelby family as they navigate gang wars, political intrigue, the rise of fascism, and the personal cost of power across three decades.

## Characters

### The Shelby Family

---

#### Thomas "Tommy" Shelby

- **Actor:** Cillian Murphy
- **Status:** Alive (series finale); fate resolved in _The Immortal Man_ (2026)
- **Type:** Antihero / Crime Boss
- **Bio:** The undisputed mastermind of the Peaky Blinders. A decorated tunneler and sniper in the First World War (Gallipoli and France), Tommy returned from the trenches with severe PTSD and an almost supernatural coldness. His strategic brilliance and total ruthlessness drive him from small-time gang leader to Member of Parliament and international criminal operator. He is diagnosed with tuberculoma (a brain lesion) in Series 6, believing he is dying — which he ultimately survives.
- **Fate:** (_The Immortal Man_) Confesses to his sister Ada that he killed Arthur — not out of mercy but out of rage and a desire to be free. Burns his own wagon as a funeral pyre. Ends with the words: _"I am free."_

---

#### Arthur Shelby Jr.

- **Actor:** Paul Anderson
- **Status:** Deceased (killed by Tommy in _The Immortal Man_)
- **Type:** Antihero
- **Bio:** Tommy's older brother and enforcer — ferociously loyal, prone to violence, and locked in a lifelong battle with addiction (morphine, cocaine). Arthur is the Peaky Blinders' muscle and second-in-command. His religious conversion under Linda's influence and his eventual friendship with Hayden Stagg represent genuine attempts at redemption, always undermined by the gravity of the family.
- **Fate:** Killed by Tommy. Tommy confesses to Ada: _"I killed him because I was full of booze and rage."_

---

#### Elizabeth "Polly" Gray (née Shelby)

- **Actor:** Helen McCrory (died April 2021; character written out of Series 6)
- **Status:** Deceased (killed off-screen between Series 5 and 6)
- **Type:** Hero / Matriarch
- **Bio:** Tommy's aunt and the Shelby family's treasurer, conscience, and spiritual anchor. Polly is a Romani woman of formidable intelligence and moral authority — she manages the family's money, reads tarot cards, and is the only person Tommy genuinely respects and fears. Her Romani faith, her grief for her lost children (Michael and a daughter), and her refusal to be merely Tommy's instrument define her arc.
- **Fate:** Killed during the IRA ambush that was meant for Mosley; avenged by Arthur in the Series 6 opening: _"Rest in peace, Pol."_

---

#### Ada Thorne (née Shelby)

- **Actor:** Sophie Rundle
- **Status:** Alive
- **Type:** Hero (moral compass)
- **Bio:** The only Shelby sister and the family's socialist conscience. Ada married communist Freddie Thorne (Series 1) against Tommy's wishes and became an activist and voice of reason throughout the series. She eventually becomes Tommy's most trusted confidante after Polly's death.

---

#### John "Johnny" Shelby

- **Actor:** Joe Cole
- **Status:** Deceased (killed in Series 4 Changretta hit)
- **Type:** Antihero
- **Bio:** The third Shelby brother — impulsive, good-natured, and the father of many children. John married Esme Lee, binding the Shelbys to the Lee Romani clan. His death at the hands of Changretta's assassins triggers the S4 vendetta arc.

---

#### Finn Shelby

- **Actor:** Harry Kirton (grown-up)
- **Status:** Alive
- **Type:** Supporting
- **Bio:** The youngest Shelby brother. Grows up inside the gang and never quite escapes Tommy's shadow.

---

### Major Supporting Characters

---

#### Alfred "Alfie" Solomons

- **Actor:** Tom Hardy
- **Status:** Alive (survived being shot by Tommy; resurfaces in Series 5 and 6)
- **Type:** Villain / Comic Relief / Unlikely Ally
- **Bio:** Leader of a Jewish gang in Camden Town, rum distiller, and operator of extraordinary verbal chaos. Alfie is simultaneously Tommy's most treacherous enemy and his most philosophically compatible companion. He speaks in long, looping, absurdist monologues that conceal genuine cunning. He betrayed Tommy to the Odd Fellows in Series 3, was shot by Tommy on a beach in Series 4, survived, and by Series 5 is living in Margate as a self-proclaimed deity, shooting at seagulls with a gun and reading the Torah.
- **Notable:** After Tommy shoots him, Alfie wakes up on the beach and thinks he is in Hell: _"If this is Hell... then it looks a lot like Margate."_ He eventually brokers the final deal in _The Immortal Man_.

---

#### Grace Shelby (née Burgess)

- **Actor:** Annabelle Wallis
- **Status:** Deceased (shot at a party; Series 3)
- **Type:** Hero (tragic)
- **Bio:** An undercover agent for Inspector Campbell sent to spy on Tommy via the Garrison pub. She falls genuinely in love with him, defects, and eventually becomes his first wife and mother of his son Charles. Her death from a bullet intended for Tommy is one of the series' defining tragedies.

---

#### Inspector/Major Chester Campbell

- **Actor:** Sam Neill
- **Status:** Deceased
- **Type:** Villain (institutional)
- **Bio:** An Ulster Protestant detective chief inspector sent by Winston Churchill from Belfast to recover a stolen cache of weapons. Campbell is rigidly moralistic and genuinely menacing — a man who uses the law as a weapon and believes the ends justify the means. He falls into an obsession with both Tommy and Grace that ultimately destroys him.

---

#### Michael Gray

- **Actor:** Finn Cole
- **Status:** Deceased (shot by Tommy; Series 6 finale)
- **Type:** Antihero → Villain
- **Bio:** Polly's biological son, raised by foster parents and reunited with the family in Series 2. Michael becomes the family's accountant and increasingly ambitious schemer, ultimately conspiring with his American wife Gina (and Gina's uncle Jack Nelson) to take control of the Shelby Company from Tommy. His final act is to attempt Tommy's assassination. Tommy kills him first.
- **Final Words:** _"I have consulted my mother... When I'm released, it is my intention to kill Tommy Shelby."_

---

#### Luca Changretta

- **Actor:** Adrien Brody
- **Status:** Deceased
- **Type:** Villain
- **Bio:** A New York Mafioso who comes to Birmingham to avenge the killing of his father Vicente by the Shelbys. Methodical, elegant, and confident in the superiority of his organization. He delivers his vendetta declaration by placing bullets on a table, one for each Shelby family member — and flicks John's bullet away as "spent." Tommy ultimately turns Alfie against him and kills him.

---

#### Sir Oswald Mosley

- **Actor:** Sam Claflin
- **Status:** Alive (Series 5–6)
- **Type:** Villain (political)
- **Bio:** Based on the real-life leader of the British Union of Fascists. Tommy's parliamentary nemesis and the primary antagonist of Series 5. Tommy attempts to assassinate him at a rally in the Series 5 finale — the plan collapses due to an informant. Mosley represents the most existential threat Tommy faces: institutional evil that cannot be beaten with guns alone.

---

#### Jessie Eden

- **Actor:** Charlie Murphy
- **Status:** Alive
- **Type:** Ally / Love Interest
- **Bio:** Based on the real Birmingham communist and trade union leader. Jessie becomes Tommy's lover and a source of genuine political inspiration. She leads factory strikes and is Tommy's most intellectually equal relationship.

## Notable Story Beats & Lore

- **The Razor Blades** — The name "Peaky Blinders" derives from the historical Birmingham gang's alleged practice of sewing razor blades into the peaks of their flat caps, blinding enemies with a headbutt. The show acknowledges this is partly myth.
- **The Stolen Guns** — Series 1 begins when a crate of BSA-manufactured rifles and a Lewis gun are accidentally taken by the Peaky Blinders from a government shipment. Churchill dispatches Inspector Campbell to recover them. Tommy uses the guns as leverage to expand his empire.
- **The Tunnelers** — Tommy and Arthur served as tunnelers at Gallipoli — men who dug underground mines toward enemy lines and set explosive charges. Tommy still hears the shovels in his nightmares: _"Sometimes it lasts all night. And I lie here and I listen to the shovels... and I pray that the sun will come up at the curtains before they break through."_
- **The Soldier's Minute** — Tommy's philosophy of living: _"In a battle, that's all you get. One minute of everything at once. And anything before is nothing. Everything after, nothing."_
- **The Narrow Boats** — The Shelby family grew up on canal narrow boats in the Black Country — poverty-stricken, Romani, and marginal. Tommy uses the canal metaphor repeatedly, notably in Series 6 when encouraging Arthur through addiction: _"Just a few more yards to go."_
- **Tommy's PTSD** — Throughout the series Tommy is haunted by his war experience. He describes lying awake hearing imaginary tunneling sounds. He self-medicates with alcohol and opium. His mother's ghost appears to him in visions in Series 6.
- **"By Order of the Peaky Blinders"** — The gang's signature phrase, used on notices, warnings, and declarations. It signals irreversibility.
- **The Tuberculoma Twist** — In Series 6, Tommy is told he has a tuberculoma and will die. He spends the series putting his affairs in order. The finale of _The Immortal Man_ reveals the diagnosis was a deliberate misread — Tommy survives, outliving nearly everyone he loved.
- **Ruby's Death** — Tommy's daughter Ruby (with Lizzie) dies of tuberculosis in Series 6. She speaks Romani words ("Tikna Mora o'Beng" — "the black dog") in her fevered death throes, a phrase that haunts Tommy for the rest of the series.
- **Polly's Romani Faith** — Polly practices Romani spiritual traditions — tarot, candles, the Evil Eye. Her faith is treated with complete seriousness by the show, not as superstition. Her visions and warnings are consistently accurate.
- **The Opening Theme** — _"Red Right Hand"_ by Nick Cave and the Bad Seeds plays over the iconic slow-motion opening sequences and has become inseparable from the show's identity. David Bowie was a devoted fan of the series; Leonard Cohen wrote a new song for Series 3.
- **Cultural Impact** — After the show aired, the name Arthur surged into the UK top 10 boys' names for the first time since the 1920s, and Ada entered the girls' top 100 for the first time in a century, per the UK Office for National Statistics.
